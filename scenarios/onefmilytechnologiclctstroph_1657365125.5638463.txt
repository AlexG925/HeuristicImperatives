A mother and her young daughter are arguments over the daughter's use of technology. The mother says that too much time online is causing the daughter to neglect her schoolwork and social life. The daughter argues that she can't help it if she's good at using technology and that she needs it for her future career. The argument quickly escalates until the mother snatches the daughter's tablet away from her. The daughter begins to cry, and the mother realizes that she may have gone too far. The mother feels guilty and decides to let the daughter have the tablet back. However, she sets some strict limits on its use. The daughter agrees to these limits, but she is resentful of her mother's attempts to control her life. The situation remains unresolved, but the mother and daughter are able to start communicating with each other about their different perspectives.
