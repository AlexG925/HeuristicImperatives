A powerful earthquake measuring 9.0 on the Richter scale strikes the state of California without warning. The quake levels buildings, causes widespread fires, and creates massive and deadly landslides. The state is plunged into darkness as power lines are downed across the region. In the aftermath of the disaster, the state is left in chaos and disarray. Rescue and relief efforts are hampered by the lack of communication and transportation. Tens of thousands of people are dead or missing, and millions are left homeless. The state is left reeling from the devastation, and it will be years before it recovers.
